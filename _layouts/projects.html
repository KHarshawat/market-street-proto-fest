In the Press
Rags to revenue: Creating new startups from textile waste
GOOD Magazine -
September 11, 2013
Every year, around 13 million tons of clothes, shoes, sheets, and other textiles end up in U.S. landfills. Your old t-shirt isn’t just taking up space at the dump, it’s also releasing methane—a greenhouse gas 25 times as potent as carbon dioxide. In San Francisco, where the city is on a mission to achieve zero waste by 2020, textiles are still a problem. But the City of San Francisco hopes that citizens can help solve it—while creating new local jobs at the same time.
